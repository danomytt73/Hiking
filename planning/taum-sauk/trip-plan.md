### 4. Animal Management & Unforeseen Events

**Status:** Not started
**Scope:**

**Wildlife:**

- Black bears: present but low-density in Mark Twain NF / St. François Mountains; no canister requirement but food hang or Ursack is best practice; settle approach before meal planning
- Copperheads: active on rocky rhyolite terrain until first hard frost; mid-to-late October is the edge of their active season — knowing what to do on encounter and what NOT to do (tourniquets, cutting/sucking) is worth a refresh
- Ticks and chiggers: underestimated October hazard in the Ozarks; chiggers persist later than most people expect; permethrin treatment of clothing and gaiters before the trip is standard; daily tick checks especially around waistband, behind knees, scalp
- Deer and turkey: no management needed; note that late October overlaps with Missouri deer season — blaze orange consideration for approach roads and any off-trail movement

**Pre-Trip: Permethrin Treatment**

- Treat socks, gaiters, pants, and shirt at least 24–48 hours before departure so clothing is fully dry before wearing
- Apply outdoors or in a well-ventilated area; keep away from cats until completely dry (permethrin is toxic to cats while wet)
- Do NOT apply directly to skin — clothing treatment only
- Permethrin bonds to fabric and survives 4–6 washes; one treatment covers the full trip
- Sawyer Permethrin Clothing & Gear spray (~$15) is the standard; treat in your driveway and hang to dry
- Pair with DEET-based repellent (20–30%) on exposed skin for full coverage during active hiking hours

**Injury / Medical:**

- Solo trip means self-rescue is the only immediate option; inReach SOS is the backstop
- Most likely events: twisted ankle on rhyolite, foot bruising, blister management, hypothermia on exposed glades in a fast-moving front
- First aid kit contents specific to this trip: blister kit (Leukotape over moleskin), SAM splint, ace bandage, tick key, emergency bivy
- Know the nearest hospital / trauma center from JSI and from the Taum Sauk area (Iron County / Reynolds County)

**Weather Events:**

- October Ozarks sees fast-moving cold fronts; temperature can drop 30°F in a few hours
- Lightning on open glades (Goggins, Proffit, Bell Mountain) is a real exposure — know descent routes off exposed ridges
- Flash flood risk in creek drainages after heavy rain; Taum Sauk Creek and East Fork Black River can rise fast; the 2005 reservoir breach history is a reminder of how quickly this terrain floods

## Skills to Develop

*Not blocking for Taum Sauk (3 days), but worth building toward longer sections and future trips*

- **Rope & knot tying** — relevant for 5-day+ sections where food hangs are essential in higher bear pressure areas; core knots: PCT two-stick hang, bowline, trucker's hitch, clove hitch; 50' of 1/8" Dyneema or 550 paracord covers most needs; YouTube channels like "Animated Knots" are the best way to learn and retain these
- **Wilderness first aid** — NOLS Wilderness First Aid (2-day course) is the practical standard for solo backpackers; covers patient assessment, splinting, evacuation decisions; worth completing before any trip 4+ days solo
- **Navigation / map & compass** — no longer a someday skill: paper map + compass is the chosen no-battery backstop for the Taum Sauk nav stack (alongside Fenix 7 + iPhone 17 Pro), so this needs to be a real, practiced capability before the trip, not just theory. Core skills to build: orienting the map to terrain (including correcting for magnetic declination — Missouri sits near the agonic line so the correction is likely small, but confirm the exact value for the Hwy 21 trailhead via [NOAA's calculator](https://www.ngdc.noaa.gov/geomag-web/) rather than assume), taking and following a bearing, triangulation/resection to fix position, pace counting for distance.
  - **Practice plan:** attend 1–2 [St. Louis Orienteering Club](https://stlouisorienteering.org/) events (Tower Grove Park / Queeny Park / Creve Coeur Park, $5–20) for structured, feedback-rich practice with real checkpoints → 2 informal printed-map + compass hikes at Rockwoods Reservation (rocky terrain, closest local analog to Taum Sauk's rhyolite tread) and Castlewood State Park (river bluffs, good terrain-association landmarks) → optional extra reps at Rock Bridge Memorial State Park's permanent 30-marker orienteering course (near Columbia, open daily) → final tabletop bearing exercise using the actual Taum Sauk topo before departure

### Trail — General Ozark Trail Planning

- **Ozark Trail Association** | ozarktrail.com — official GPX downloads, section maps, trail services directory (shuttles, B&Bs)
- **Ben's Hikes — Taum Sauk Section** | benshikes.weebly.com/taum-sauk-section — clean section breakdown, good overview of the three sub-segments

### Photography — Location Research

- **Greg Matchick Photography — Johnson's Shut-Ins** | gregmatchickphotography.com/johnsons-shut-ins — serious landscape work at JSI; study for light angle, composition approach, fall color timing

- **"Landscape Photography" by Guy Tal** — deliberate, unhurried approach to landscape work that aligns with your slow-morning philosophy; not a technical manual, more a way of seeing

- **Outdoor Photographer magazine** | outdoorphotographer.com — fall issue (Sept/Oct) typically has relevant landscape and backcountry photography features

### Lightweight Backpacking

- **Zpacks** | zpacks.com — product pages for Arc Blast, Arc Haul, Duplex tent, Plume quilt; read specs and lead times before ordering
- **Andrew Skurka's website** | andrewskurka.com — gear lists, food planning, systems thinking for multi-day trips; highly practical
- **"Ultralight Backpackin' Tips" by Mike Clelland** — illustrated, fast read; good primer on UL philosophy before the big three chat
- **r/ultralight** | reddit.com/r/ultralight — gear reviews, trip reports, Zpacks field reports; search "Zpacks Duplex review" and "Ozark Trail" for community-sourced intel

### Meal Planning

- **"The Ultimate Hiker's Gear Guide" by Andrew Skurka** — has a full chapter on backcountry nutrition and food weight; the calorie-per-ounce framework is essential
- **Trail Recipes** | trailrecipes.com — DIY dehydrated meal database; useful if you want to go beyond freeze-dried
- **Backpacking Chef** | backpackingchef.com — dehydrator-based recipes; good if you want to prep your own food

### Geology & Natural History (makes the photography richer)

- **Missouri Department of Conservation — St. François Mountains** | mdc.mo.gov — geology, ecology, and natural history of the rhyolite and igneous glade system you'll be moving through
- **"Ozarks: Land and Life" by Milton Rafferty** — regional natural history; the chapter on the St. François Mountains is the best accessible account of why this landscape looks and feels the way it does
