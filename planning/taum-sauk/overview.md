## Project Overview

Dan is section-hiking the full Ozark Trail (OT) in Missouri, with landscape and nature photography as a co-equal goal. He's based near St. Louis. The full OT is divided into five logical sections tackled over multiple years. Each section gets its own dedicated chat.

The first section in active planning is the **Taum Sauk Section**: 35 miles, ~4,250' gain, hiked solo north to south (Hwy 21 → Johnson's Shut-Ins State Park) over 3 days in mid-to-late October for peak fall color.

## Key Trail Intelligence

### Water Sources (N→S, reliability in October)

- Claybaugh Creek, ~mile 2–3 — seasonal, fill early
- Taum Sauk Creek, ~mile 18–19 — reliable; search upstream if needed
- Padfield Branch, ~mile 22 — most reliable mid-section; spring behind old house
- Walker Branch, ~mile 24 — rarely flowing, do not depend on it
- East Fork Black River / footbridge — reliable
- Scour area stream, ~mile 33 — pick up last liter before final descent

Carry 3–4L capacity minimum. 6–8 mile dry stretches exist.

### Key Photo Locations & Timing

- Bell Mountain / Lindsey Mountain glades — morning light, Day 1
- Goggins Mountain glade — golden hour / sunset, Day 1 (~6:15–6:30 PM CDT in October)
- Devil's Tollgate — mid-morning, Day 2 (rhyolite canyon, fall color overhead)
- Mina Sauk Falls — mid-morning, Day 2 (Missouri's tallest at 132', likely trickle in October)
- Proffit Mountain east glade — sunrise, Day 3 (~7:15–7:30 AM CDT)
- The Scour — open sky morning light, Day 3 (2005 reservoir breach recovery landscape)
- Johnson's Shut-Ins — 9–11 AM, Day 3 (rhyolite chutes, reflection pools; polarizer essential)

### Navigation Attention Zone

Between miles 3–4 from Hwy A (Bell Mountain area southbound): trail disappears in forest sections, few blazes, many fallen trees. Look for cairns on glades. Garmin GPX is the lifeline here.

### Camping Rules

- Primitive dispersed, 100' from trail
- No camping on glades (ecological fragility)
- No camping within 2 miles of JSI parking lot
- Backcountry registration required via mostateparks.com

## Animal Management Notes (pre-work done)

- **Bears:** low-density in Mark Twain NF; no canister requirement; food hang or Ursack best practice
- **Copperheads:** active until first hard frost; mid-to-late October is the edge; no tourniquets, no cutting/sucking
- **Ticks/chiggers:** active into October; permethrin treat clothing 24–48 hrs before departure
- **Deer season:** late October overlaps archery season; blaze orange for approach roads
- **Permethrin:** Sawyer spray on socks, gaiters, pants, shirt; apply outdoors; toxic to cats while wet; survives 4–6 washes
- **First aid kit:** Leukotape + moleskin (blisters), SAM splint, ace bandage, tick key, emergency bivy, inReach SOS
