# Ozark Trail Taum Sauk Section — Gear to Buy

Trip: Hwy 21 → Johnson's Shut-Ins | 35 miles | 3 days | Mid-to-late October

---

## To Purchase

- [ ] **Light tripod or GorillaPod** — for Proffit Mountain sunrise camp and JSI morning shut-ins work; keep under 1 lb; Joby GorillaPod 3K or similar
  - *Moved from Still Evaluating — committed purchase*
- [ ] **Sawyer Squeeze or Sawyer Mini water filter** — essential for creek/spring water; Padfield Branch and Taum Sauk Creek are primary sources; long dry stretches make a reliable filter non-negotiable
  - Sawyer Squeeze recommended over Mini for flow rate on a 3-day trip with high water demand
  - Bring a backup chemical option (Aquatabs) as redundancy
- [ ] **Satellite communicator (SOS/messaging)** — confirmed: Fenix 7 is the **base model, not Pro**, so it has no satellite hardware and can't pair as an inReach Remote. Decision simplifies to: buy a standalone **Garmin inReach Mini 2** for satellite SOS/messaging, or skip satcom and rely on cell coverage + a shared itinerary
  - Fenix 7 (base) already supports loading GPX courses for on-wrist turn-by-turn/breadcrumb navigation — likely makes a separate eTrex 32x unnecessary regardless of the satcom decision
  - GPX already downloaded from OTA; load onto the Fenix 7 via Garmin Connect
  - Trail navigation gets tricky in the Bell Mountain area southbound (few blazes, disappearing tread) — good reason to have a real dedicated nav device rather than relying on phone alone
  - inReach Mini 2 remains the strong recommendation for solo SOS/messaging given no cell coverage on most of this route
- [ ] **Sawyer Permethrin Clothing & Gear Spray** (~$15) — treat socks, gaiters, pants, shirt 24–48 hrs before departure; apply outdoors, dry fully before wearing or storing near pets
  - Pairs with DEET repellent (20–30%) on exposed skin during hiking hours
  - One treatment survives 4–6 washes; covers the full trip
- [ ] **Power solution for 3 days** — options below; pick one:
  - **Solar panel + battery bank combo** (e.g. BioLite SolarPanel 5+ or Anker 625 Solar): best for 3-day trips with daylight hiking time; charges bank while you hike
  - **High-capacity battery bank only** (e.g. Anker 733, 10,000 mAh): lighter/simpler if you can pre-calculate charge needs; no moving parts
  - Devices to keep charged: Garmin inReach (if purchased), camera battery, phone (emergency/navigation backup)
  - Note: Garmin inReach Mini 2 has ~14 day battery life on default tracking — may not need daily charging

## Explore in Separate Chats

- **Boots** — currently have Keen boots; considering a wider toe box option; evaluate trail runners vs. boots for rocky Ozark rhyolite tread
  - Rock plate note: a rock plate is a stiff insert (or built-in midsole layer) that prevents sharp rhyolite edges from bruising the ball of your foot on sustained rocky tread — highly relevant on this trail
  - Brands to explore: Altra (wide toe box), Hoka, Salomon, La Sportiva
  - Also evaluate: do current Keens have enough ankle support and rock protection for this terrain?
- **Photography kit** — filters, camera body/lens decisions for landscape/water work
- **Big Three camping gear** — sleep system, shelter, pack; explore Zpacks specifically (Arc Blast / Arc Haul packs, Duplex tent, sleeping quilts); Zpacks well-suited to October Ozarks given low precipitation odds and temp range
