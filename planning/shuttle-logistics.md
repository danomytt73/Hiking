# Ozark Trail Section Hiking — Shuttle & Transportation Logistics

General transportation strategy that applies across all OT sections. This is a
cross-cutting reference; section-specific shuttle decisions live in each
section's folder.

## Context

Dan is section-hiking the full Ozark Trail (OT) in Missouri over multiple years, based near St. Louis. Trip planning is well underway — the Taum Sauk Section (35 miles, 3 days, mid-to-late October) is the first planned section with shuttle already resolved (friend pickup at Johnson's Shut-Ins).

## Topic Discussed

General transportation logistics for section hiking: how to get from trail end back to car at trail start.

## Options Covered

- Shuttle services (OT lacks established infrastructure; OTA and Facebook groups are primary resources)
- Hitching (works but rural Missouri = low traffic)
- Two-car shuttle with a partner (most practical for OT given frequent road crossings)
- Public transit (very limited for OT)
- Rental car at endpoint
- Trail angel networks
- Friend/family drop-off (viable since Dan is local)
- Flip-flop approach

## Key OT-Specific Notes

- No dedicated shuttle services like more famous trails
- Canoe outfitters in the Current River/Eleven Point corridor sometimes offer shuttles
- Frequent road crossings every 20-30 miles make shuttle logistics more flexible
- OTA and active OT Facebook communities are the primary coordination resources
